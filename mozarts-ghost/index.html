How I Fell in Love With AI

Long before “AI tools” were a normal thing, I saw a tiny glimpse of the future in a movie… and it stuck with me.

In 1995, I watched The Net. There’s a moment where she clicks, orders pizza, and it felt like magic — not the pizza, the idea.

It was the first time I remember thinking: “One day, we’ll really be able to do things like that — instantly, from anywhere — like the computer understands what you want.”

That little scene became a quiet promise to myself. I didn’t know what the technology would be called, or how it would work. I just knew I wanted to live long enough to see it.

And now here we are: tools that can write, search, reason, build — and sometimes surprise you. Not because they’re perfect, but because they make the future feel close enough to touch.
